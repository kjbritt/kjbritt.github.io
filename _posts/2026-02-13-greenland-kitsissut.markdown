The world saw a number of important innovations 4,500 years ago. In the Nile Valley, ancient Egpytians constructed the pyramids at Giza. On the Salisbury Plain, Brittons erected Stonehenge. And in the Arctic, Paleo-Inuit people used skin-on-wood boats to access remote islands off the coast of Greenland.
