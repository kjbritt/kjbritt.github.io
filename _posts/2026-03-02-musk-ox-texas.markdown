Today, surviving wild musk oxen (_Ovibos moschatus_) are restricted to northern latitudes, living in places like Arctic Canada, Greenland, Alaska, Scandinavia and Siberia. During the last Ice Age, however, musk oxen and their close relatives were able to range over a much larger portion of the globe. In North America, fossil evidence suggests extinct species of musk ox inhabited North America from Alaska down to Mexico. 

Texas is a state famous for fossil-finds, like the dinosaur footprints at [Dinosaur Vallery State Park](https://kjbritt.github.io/paleontology/2026/01/27/dinosaur-valley-tx.html) or the _in situ_ mammoth skeletons at Waco Mammoth National Monument.
